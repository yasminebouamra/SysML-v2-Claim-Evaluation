Mission Requirements

- The OT-UAV should transport the Organ Carrier from a donor hospital to the recipient hospital
- The OT-UAV should have a maximum endurance of XX hours on a fully charged battery
- The OT-UAV should house the Organ Carrier, battery, and communications systems
- The OT-UAV should transport the organ within the viable time window for the type of organ being transported

Operational Requirements

- The OT-UAV should have fully autonomous take-off, flight, and landing capabilities
- The OT-UAV should have a human-controller override
- The OT-UAV should be resilient to operate in a wide range of flight-safe weather conditions
- The OT-UAV should be operable during the day and the night
- The OT-UAV should be operable beyond line-of-sight (BLOS)
- The OT-UAV should comply with all FAA regulations

Organ Carrier Requirements

- The Organ Carrier should house an auxiliary battery, Organ Monitoring System, and the Organ
- The Organ Carrier should be structurally resilient to OT-UAV ground and inflight temperatures, forces, and vibrations
- The Organ Carrier should house the Organ in organ-specific requirements for orientation, temperature, forces, and vibrations
- The Organ Monitoring System should monitor and report the temporal data for temperature, forces, vibrations, and location of the Organ Carrier
- The Organ Monitoring System communications channel should not interfere with OT-UAV operations communications channel

Rescue System Requirements

- The Organ Carrier should be equipped with a Rescue System that will protect the Organ and safely land on the ground in case of a midair malfunction
- The Rescue System should deliver the Organ Carrier gently to the ground to protect the Organ
- The Carrier Mount should disengage the Organ Carrier in case of a midair malfunction
- The Organ Monitoring System should monitor and report the temporal temperature, forces, vibrations, and location of the Organ Carrier in case of a midair malfunction



Carrier Mount Requirements

The OT-UAV should have a Carrier Mount as an interface to the Organ Carrier
The Carrier Mount should be structurally resilient to house the Organ Carrier
The Carrier Mount should be structurally resilient to temperature, forces, and vibrations of the OT-UAV during ground and inflight operations



Security Requirements

Access to the Organ Carrier should only be granted to eligible individuals to prevent theft

Mass Requirements

The actual mass shall be less than or equal to the required mass
