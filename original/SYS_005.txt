Orion Spacecraft Requirements

- The Orion spacecraft should be capable of safely carrying astronauts on deep space exploration missions

Crew Module Requirements

- The Orion spacecraft should have a crew module designed for astronauts to live and work in
- The crew module should be able to accommodate four crew members for up to 21 days without docking with another spacecraft
- The crew module should have a propulsion system capable of providing full control of crew module translation and rotation
- The crew module should provide a safe habitat for crew members through launch, in-orbit operations, landing, and recovery
- The top of the crew module should have a parachute system to be deployed after re-entering Earth's atmosphere that slows down the module to a safe speed for splashdown

Service Module Requirements

- The Orion spacecraft should have a service module that provides power, propulsion, and critical supplies
- The consumable storage system of the service module should provide potable water, nitrogen, and oxygen to the crew module
- The service module should unfurl its solar arrays once Orion is above the atmosphere
- The solar arrays should charge batteries in the crew module
- The Orion spacecraft should operate off of battery power if the solar arrays cannot be depended upon
- The service module should propel Orion after the spacecraft separates from the upper stage of the SLS rocket
The service module should maintain the course of Orion as it travels to the Moon and back
- The service module should detach from the crew module before it enters Earth's atmosphere
- The service module's thermal control system should keep the equipment and crew members at a comfortable temperature

Launch Abort System (LAS) Requirements

- The Orion spacecraft should have a launch abort system that can pull the spacecraft and crew to safety in the event of an emergency during launch or ascent to orbit
- The LAS should pull the spacecraft away from the rocket and position the module for a safe landing
- The abort motor should be able to pull the crew module away from the rocket
- The attitude control motor should be able to steer and orient the capsule precisely
- The jettison motor should be able to ignite to separate the LAS from the crew module prior to parachute deployment
- The launch abort system (LAS) should be able to activate within milliseconds

Communication and Navigation Requirements

- The Orion spacecraft should be capable of communicating and navigating beyond the range of GPS and above communication satellites in Earth orbit

Radiation Shielding Requirements

- The Orion spacecraft should have sufficient radiation shielding to protect its internals

Heat Shielding Requirements

- The Orion spacecraft should have sufficient heat shielding to protect its internals during high-speed re-entry into Earth's atmosphere
- The Orion spacecraft should have sufficient ablative heat shielding to endure temperatures of nearly 5,000 degrees Fahrenheit

Flight Software Requirements

- The Orion primary flight computer software should be fully automated such that it can execute the mission and handle potential faults
