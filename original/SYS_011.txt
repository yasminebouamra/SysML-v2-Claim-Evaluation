Standard Drone Engine Requirements

- The engine should provide enough power to lift the drone off the ground and keep it in the air

- The power output should be appropriate for the size and weight of the drone


- The engine should be efficient, allowing the drone to fly for an extended period on a single charge

The engine should be designed to minimize energy loss through friction and heat


The engine should be able to withstand the stress of continuous use and exposure to the elements

The engine should be able to operate in a wide range of temperatures and weather conditions


The engine should be reliable, with minimal downtime or maintenance requirements

The engine should be designed with high-quality components and undergo rigorous testing before deployment


The engine should be lightweight, allowing for maximum payload capacity and longer flight times

The engine should be easy to control, allowing for precise maneuverability and responsiveness to commands

The engine should be compatible with the drone's power supply and electronic components

The engine should be designed with safety in mind, minimizing the risk of injury or damage in case of malfunction

The engine should have fail-safe mechanisms to prevent accidents and minimize damage in case of emergency


The engine should be cost-effective, balancing performance and quality with affordability

The engine should provide value for money, with a low cost of ownership and maintenance



Low Noise Drone Engine Requirements (Additional)

The engine should produce minimal noise to avoid disturbing the environment or attracting unwanted attention

Drone Battery Requirements

The battery should have adequate weight specifications for the drone design

The battery should have sufficient maximum capacity to support the intended flight duration

The battery should monitor and report current capacity levels (full, medium, low, critical)

The battery cells should store electrical energy efficiently

Battery cells should typically be Lithium Polymer (LiPo) or Lithium-Ion (Li-Ion) cells


The Battery Management System (BMS) should manage the charging and discharging of the battery cells

The BMS should ensure that cells are charged and discharged correctly and safely


The protection circuit should protect the battery from overcharging, over-discharging, and short circuits

The power connector should deliver electrical energy from the battery to the drone

The charging interface should allow the battery to be charged safely and efficiently

The battery indicator should display the remaining battery charge

The indicator should allow the pilot to monitor battery level and plan flight time accordingly


The communication interface should allow the battery to communicate with the drone's flight controller

The interface should provide battery information such as voltage, current, and temperature to the flight controller


The firmware should control the charging and discharging of the battery cells

The firmware should ensure that the battery operates safely and efficiently



Drone System Requirements

The drone should have appropriate mass specifications based on design requirements

The drone should support multiple operational states: off, standby, charging, ready, and flying

The drone should support state transitions based on control signals (switch on/off, activate/deactivate, start/stop charging, start/stop flying)

The drone should have a power management system that monitors and controls battery status

The drone should support variable numbers of engines (4 or 6) based on design configuration

The drone should have a body structure with defined spatial dimensions and color options

The drone should have a flight control system appropriate for the engine configuration

The drone should include safety modules for safe operation

The drone should support autonomous flight capabilities

The drone should have navigation systems including GPS module, inertial measurement unit, and barometer

The drone should have a communication system including radio transmitter, receiver, and antenna

The drone should have sensors including camera and obstacle avoidance sensors

The drone should have proper electrical connections between the drone control unit, power management module, and battery
