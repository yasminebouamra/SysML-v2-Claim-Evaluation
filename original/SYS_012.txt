Mass Requirements

The actual mass of any vehicle component should be less than or equal to the required mass

Reliability Requirements

The actual reliability of any vehicle component should be greater than or equal to the required reliability

Vehicle Specification Requirements
Vehicle Mass Requirement

The total mass of a vehicle should be less than or equal to 2000 kg
The total mass calculation should include a full tank of gas
The total mass should be calculated as the sum of dry mass, cargo mass, and fuel mass



Fuel Economy Requirements

The vehicle should meet fuel economy requirements for different driving conditions

City Fuel Economy Requirement

The vehicle should obtain fuel economy of at least 25 miles per gallon in city driving conditions
This requirement assumes a cargo mass of at least 500 kg



Highway Fuel Economy Requirement

The vehicle should obtain fuel economy of at least 30 miles per gallon in highway driving conditions
This requirement assumes a cargo mass of at least 500 kg



Engine Requirements
Torque Generation Requirement

The engine should generate torque as a function of RPM as shown in Table 1

Drive Power Interface Requirement

The engine should transfer its generated torque to the transmission via the clutch interface

Fuel Tank Requirements

The fuel tank should maintain fuel mass less than or equal to the maximum fuel capacity

Vehicle Operational Requirements
Power Provision

The vehicle should provide power to the wheels through the drivetrain system
The engine should generate torque based on fuel commands
The transmission should amplify the engine torque
The driveshaft should transfer torque from the transmission to the axle assembly
The axle assembly should distribute torque to the wheels



Self-Test Capability

The vehicle should be capable of performing self-test procedures

Parking Brake

The vehicle should have the capability to apply a parking brake

Temperature Monitoring

The vehicle should sense and monitor temperature conditions

Vehicle State Requirements
Operating States

The vehicle should support the following operating states: off, starting, and on
The vehicle should transition from off to starting when:
A vehicle start signal is received
The brake pedal is depressed
A start signal is sent to the controller


The vehicle should transition from starting to on when a vehicle-on signal is received
The vehicle should transition from on to off when a vehicle-off signal is received
When entering the "on" state, the vehicle should perform a self-test
While in the "on" state, the vehicle should provide power
When exiting the "on" state, the vehicle should apply the parking brake
While in the "on" state, the electrical power should be less than or equal to 500 watts



Health States

The vehicle should monitor health conditions and support the following health states: normal, maintenance, and degraded
The vehicle should continuously sense temperature
The vehicle should transition from normal to maintenance at the scheduled maintenance time
The vehicle should transition from normal to degraded when temperature exceeds the maximum threshold
An over-temperature signal should be sent to the controller


The vehicle should transition from maintenance to normal when a return-to-normal signal is received
The vehicle should transition from degraded to normal when a return-to-normal signal is received



Vehicle Controller Requirements
Controller Operating States

The vehicle controller should support the following operating states: off and on
The controller should transition from off to on when a start signal is received
The controller should transition from on to off when an off signal is received



Drivetrain Efficiency Requirements

The rear axle assembly should have a drivetrain efficiency of at least 0.6 (60%)

Wheel Requirements

The rear wheels should have defined diameter specifications

Fuel Economy Analysis Requirements

The fuel economy analysis should determine whether the vehicle design configuration can satisfy the fuel economy requirements
The analysis should calculate fuel economy based on a nominal driving scenario
The analysis should use straight-line dynamics calculations considering:
Engine peak horsepower
Vehicle mass
Time intervals
Position and velocity changes


The analysis should include fuel consumption analysis based on power versus time data
